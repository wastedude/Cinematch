# CineMatch — User Guide

> Swipe movies with a friend. When you both like the same film — it's a match.

---

## What is CineMatch?

CineMatch is a free web app that helps two people decide what movie to watch together. Each person picks their favourite genres, then you both swipe through a shared deck of 15 movies. When you both swipe right on the same film, you get an instant "It's a Match!" notification.

No account needed. No app to install. Just open the link and start swiping.

---

## How to Play

### Step 1 — Create a room

1. Go to the CineMatch website
2. Tap **Create a room**
3. You'll be taken to a room with a unique 6-character code (e.g. `XQJK72`)

### Step 2 — Invite your partner

Share your room with whoever you're watching with. You can:
- Tap **Copy link** to copy the room URL directly
- Or tell them the 6-character code — they can type it on the home screen and tap **Join room**

Both of you need to be on the room page before the fun begins.

### Step 3 — Pick your genres

Each person picks up to **3 genres** they're in the mood for (Action, Comedy, Horror, etc.). Your choices are private — your partner picks independently on their device.

Once you tap **Let's go →**, you'll wait briefly while the other person also picks theirs.

### Step 4 — Swipe!

Once both people have picked genres, a shared deck of 15 movies is generated — blended from both your tastes.

- **Swipe right** (or tap the ❤️ button) to like a movie
- **Swipe left** (or tap the ✕ button) to pass

You'll see the movie title, year, and rating on each card.

### Step 5 — It's a Match!

When you both like the same movie at the same time, a **"It's a Match!"** screen pops up instantly on both devices. If the movie has a trailer, you can watch it right from the match screen.

Tap **Keep swiping** to continue through the rest of the deck.

### Step 6 — See all your matches

After all 15 movies have been swiped, you'll see a list of every film you both liked — with links to watch their trailers.

---

## Tips

- **Genre mixing works in your favour** — the deck blends both people's genres, so you might discover films neither of you would have found alone
- **You can't change your genres** once submitted, so choose carefully
- **The deck is the same for both of you** — you're swiping the exact same 15 movies
- **Matches are instant** — as soon as both of you swipe right, the notification fires in real time

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I need to create an account?**
No. CineMatch requires no sign-up, no login, and no personal information. Your device is identified anonymously.

**Can more than 2 people join a room?**
No — rooms are designed for exactly 2 people. A third device that joins can view the room but won't be assigned a swipe slot.

**What if we don't get any matches?**
You'll see a "No matches this time" screen. Try again with different genres — or accept that your tastes are gloriously incompatible.

**Does it work on mobile?**
Yes. CineMatch is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it works in any modern browser on any device. On Android with Chrome, you can also tap **Add to Home Screen** to install it like a native app.

**Is it free?**
Yes, completely free. No ads, no subscriptions.

**What movie data does it use?**
CineMatch uses [The Movie Database (TMDB)](https://www.themoviedb.org) for all movie information, posters, and trailer links.

## Installing as an App (Optional)

CineMatch can be installed on your device for a native app-like experience:

**Android (Chrome):**
1. Open CineMatch in Chrome
2. Tap the three-dot menu
3. Tap **Add to Home screen**

**iPhone/iPad (Safari):**
1. Open CineMatch in Safari
2. Tap the Share button (box with arrow)
3. Tap **Add to Home Screen**

**Desktop (Chrome/Edge):**
1. Look for the install icon in the address bar
2. Click **Install**

## Privacy

- CineMatch stores only an anonymous random ID in your browser's local storage to identify your device within a room
- Room data (genres, swipes, matches) is stored in a secure database and tied to your anonymous room code
- No personal data, email, or account information is ever collected
- Movie data is provided by TMDB and is subject to [TMDB's terms](https://www.themoviedb.org/documentation/api/terms-of-use)
